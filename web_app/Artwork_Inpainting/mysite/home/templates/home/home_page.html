Artworks and historical documents are vulnerable to damage from environmental conditions, accidents, or vandalism, threatening their integrity and accessibility. The recent vandalism of the Mona Lisa highlights the urgent need for novel art restoration techniques. Traditional restoration methods are resource-intensive, underscoring the necessity for alternative approaches like deep learning.

This project aims to develop powerful convolutional neural network (CNN) algorithms to assess and reconstruct damaged images, specifically targeting the removal of artificially added multicolour lines. Our goal is to provide scalable and efficient tools for preserving cultural heritage, benefiting galleries, museums, and other cultural institutions.

By leveraging deep learning, we seek to contribute to the preservation of valuable cultural artifacts for future generations.
